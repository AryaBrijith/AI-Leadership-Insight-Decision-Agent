ADOBE - Q3 2023 QUARTERLY REPORT

QUARTER OVERVIEW
Q3 2023 revenue: $1.12 billion, up 16% versus Q3 2022 ($965 million).
Gross margin: 42.1%, compared to 39.8% in Q3 2022.
EBITDA: $290 million, a 21% increase YoY.

SEGMENT PERFORMANCE - Q3 2023
Technology Division: $385 million (+31% YoY) — strongest quarter on record. SaaS ARR crossed $500M milestone.
Product Sales: $512 million (+8% YoY) — moderate growth; volume gains offset by pricing pressure in EMEA.
Services: $223 million (+19% YoY) — professional services demand remained robust.

UNDERPERFORMING AREAS
The Logistics Department reported Q3 revenue of $78 million against a target of $95 million — a 18% miss. Key issues:
- Driver shortage worsened in July-August peak season
- Fuel surcharges increased operating costs by 12%
- Legacy fleet technology limiting route optimization

The HR Department flagged that voluntary attrition in the Engineering team reached 22% in Q3, higher than the annual average of 18%, which is impacting product delivery timelines.

KEY RISKS HIGHLIGHTED IN Q3
Risk 1 - Concentration Risk: Top 3 customers represent 38% of Technology Division revenue. Loss of any single account would be material.
Risk 2 - FX Exposure: USD strength impacted EMEA revenue by approximately $18M in reported terms.
Risk 3 - Regulatory: GDPR compliance gap identified in the customer data pipeline; remediation estimated at $12M, expected completion Q1 2024.
Risk 4 - Supply Chain: Semiconductor lead times extended to 22 weeks, up from 14 weeks in Q2, threatening H1 2024 hardware product launches.
Risk 5 - Competitive Pressure: Two new entrants in the SaaS market offering comparable features at 20-30% lower price points.

OPERATIONAL UPDATES
- New warehouse automation system deployed in 3 of 8 facilities; expected to reduce labor costs by 15% when fully rolled out.
- Customer satisfaction score (CSAT) improved to 87% from 82% in Q2.
- R&D spend: $145 million (13% of revenue), focused on AI-powered product features.

OUTLOOK FOR Q4 2023
Management guides Q4 revenue in the range of $1.15 - $1.20 billion.
Key watch items: holiday season demand, supply chain stabilization, and SaaS enterprise deal pipeline ($340M in active negotiations).
